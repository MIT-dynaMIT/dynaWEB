What is dynaMIT?

We are a completely free, week-long summer day program
held at the MIT campus for students in 6th to 9th grade.
We teach a variety of topics, ranging from chemistry and
biology to physics, music, and computer science.
Read about our mission and how you can apply!

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide students from low-income families with an opportunity to explore and experience science and engineering through hands-on experiments and challenges with their peers.

Students

Students in 6th-9th grades next year are eligible for this year's program. DynaMIT program dates are: August 14th to 18th for rising 6th and 7th grade students, and August 21st-25th for rising 8th and 9th grade students

  • Apply for DynaMIT!

Mentors: MIT Students

Throughout each week, MIT undergraduate and graduate students serve as mentors to inspire the students and to build interest in STEM fields. Their goal is to empower students to design and innovate beyond the summer.

  • Become a Mentor!

Board

DynaMIT is entirely student-run. Throughout the school year, MIT undergrads come up with curriculum and organize logistics so that dynaMIT can happen every summer. Read how you can join us!
